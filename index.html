Structured for Legal Recognition

Mithaq agreements are drafted in alignment with Islamic marital jurisprudence and structured to meet documentation standards recognized by judicial bodies in the UAE and across the GCC.

Why This Matters

Prevent Conflict Before It Begins

Ambiguity in marital expectations is a leading source of post-marriage dispute. A clearly documented prenuptial agreement defines obligations, rights, and expectations before the marriage contract is concluded.

Align Expectations Clearly

Financial arrangements, custodial considerations, and personal boundaries — documented with care, reviewed together, and signed with mutual understanding. The platform guides both parties through a structured, legally-informed process.

Digitally Secure & Court-Ready

Every agreement is timestamped, encrypted, and stored with verifiable integrity. Should a document ever be required in a legal or judicial context, its authenticity can be independently confirmed.

The Process

Four structured steps to a complete, signed agreement.

01

Define Terms Before Marriage

Both parties complete a guided, structured form covering financial matters, rights, obligations, and any agreed conditions permitted under Islamic jurisprudence.

02

Mutual Review & Agreement

Both parties receive a formal document for independent review. Each party may request amendments through the platform before proceeding. No signature may be applied under duress.

03

Digital Signature

Identity-verified digital signatures are applied by each party. The platform records the time, device, and location metadata of each signature event in an immutable audit trail.

04

Secure Storage & Legal Documentation

The finalized agreement is encrypted, timestamped, and stored in a tamper-proof vault. Certified copies may be obtained at any time for legal, notarial, or court-submission purposes.

Legal & Sharia Compliance

Built for Legal and Sharia Compliance

Mithaq is developed in close consultation with Islamic scholars and legal practitioners in the UAE. The platform does not offer legal advice; it provides a structured, verified documentation framework within which couples may formally record their agreed terms.

Sharia-Aware Framework

Aligned with Islamic Marital Principles

All agreement templates are structured to respect the foundational rights and obligations established in Islamic law, including mahr, nafaqah, and conditions permissible within Hanbali, Shafi'i, Maliki, and Hanafi schools of jurisprudence.

Supports Documentation Requirements

Agreements are formatted to meet the documentation standards required by the UAE Ministry of Justice and Personal Status Courts, and may be submitted as supplementary documentation to the official marriage registration process.

Transparent and Voluntary Framework

The platform is designed to prevent coercion. Each party signs independently, with a mandatory review period. Agreements may not be executed by proxy without explicit documented authorization.

Structured for Legal Recognition

Document structure, language, and formatting comply with requirements for legal instruments. Third-party notarization is supported where required by applicable jurisdiction.

Mithaq is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. All agreements are documentation tools. Users are advised to consult a qualified legal practitioner in their jurisdiction.

Document Security

Infrastructure Built for Sensitive Documents.

Marriage agreements contain some of the most personally sensitive information a couple will ever commit to writing. Security and data integrity are treated as foundational requirements, not optional features.

End-to-End Encryption

All data in transit and at rest is encrypted using AES-256. No Mithaq personnel may access the content of any agreement without explicit user authorization.

Tamper-Proof Document Storage

Documents are stored using content-addressed, cryptographically-verifiable storage. Any alteration to a stored agreement is immediately detectable.

Timestamped Agreements

Each signature event is recorded with a certified timestamp from a trusted timestamping authority, establishing an irrefutable chronological record.

Digital Verification Integrity

Every finalized agreement carries a unique verification code. Third parties — including courts, notaries, and legal counsel — may independently verify authenticity without accessing the full document.
